Whole Numbers

In grade 5-6, you'll work with whole numbers up to at least 6 digits.

Key Concepts:

Vertical colum in Mathematics refers to the numbers or other elements in a line going from top to bottom. This method is commonly used in :

  • Column Additions

Example:

       98 615
    + 103 625
    __________
       202 240
  • Column Subtraction

    • Example:

                   
                198 615
            -   103 625
            __________
                94 990

    In this example, we have to borrow from the 8 in the tens column. The 8 becomes a 7 and the 1 in the hundreds column becomes an 11.

    We then subtract 11 - 2 = 9, 7 - 2 = 5, and 8 - 6 = 2.

  • Multiplication

    • Example:

                    32
            x   25
            __________
                160
            +   640
            __________
                800

    In this example, we multiply 32 by 5 to get 160. Then we multiply 32 by 2 (which is actually 20) to get 640. Finally, we add the two products together.

  • Division

    • Example:

                    224/2
                112

    In this example, we divide 224 by 2 to get 112.

Numbers are align based on our place values

Fractions

Fractions represent parts of a whole.

Key Concepts:

  • Identifying fractions
  • Equivalent fractions
  • Comparing fractions
  • Adding and subtracting fractions with same denominator
  • Introduction to decimal fractions

Example: 3/4 + 1/4 = 4/4 = 1

Geometry

Study of shapes and their properties.

Key Concepts:

  • 2D shapes (triangles, quadrilaterals, circles)
  • 3D objects (cubes, rectangular prisms, pyramids)
  • Lines of symmetry
  • Angles (right, acute, obtuse)
  • Perimeter and area of rectangles

Example: Perimeter of rectangle = 2 × (length + width)

Measurement

Working with different units of measurement.

Key Concepts:

  • Length (mm, cm, m, km)
  • Mass (g, kg)
  • Capacity (ml, l)
  • Time (reading clocks, calculating duration)
  • Converting between units

Example: 1km = 1000m, 1m = 100cm, 1cm = 10mm
